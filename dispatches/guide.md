# Dispatch Guide

A dispatch is a firsthand account from a founder who's been somewhere. Not a travel blog post. Not a success story. A specific situation — written for the next founder about to face the same thing.

## What makes a good dispatch

Specificity. The bus route that saved you $60. The neighborhood where accommodation is half the price. The thing nobody tells you about that conference until you're already there.

Write it for the founder who's about to be in your shoes.

## What to cover

No rigid structure. But here are questions worth answering:

- Where were you, and why?
- What situation did you face?
- What did you figure out?
- What would you tell the founder who comes next?

If numbers help, include them. If a photo makes it clearer, add it. If you made a mistake, say so — that's often the most useful part.

## Format and naming

Write in Markdown. Name your file descriptively:

`2025-buenos-aires-sebastian-pabon.md`

Length: as long as it needs to be, no longer.

## One last thing

Your dispatch doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be honest.
